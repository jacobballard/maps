MAPS

One mistake Cairo talks about is the inability of a map to accurately represent an area. The Earth is obviously not a flat surface like a map appears to be, a lot of data is lost in making it like this. This is because of projection, the image distorts when trying to put it as a picture.

Another mistake has to do with properly representing size. Cairo says that the sizes of circles representing different quantities could be off if you are not careful choosing a size. This mistake is commonly made by doubling the diameter of a circle rather than its area.
